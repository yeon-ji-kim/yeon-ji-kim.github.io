Overview

Compared to our life before COVID-19, students and teachers experience more unbearable stress and emotional and psychological difficulties such as depression and anxiety. The goal of this project was to create a web application for students and teachers under school life with COVID-19 to connect with others and relieve their depression.

Challenges

During COVID-19, having time with our friends has become difficult, especially for students. According to a survey of 10,499 teenage students and teachers in Daegu, Korea, they have been experiencing more emotional and psychological difficulties, such as stress experiences and depression and anxiety, due to COVID-19. For their depression, students mentioned: ‚Äúnot being able to have time with friends at school.‚Äù Teachers were also having difficulties in managing students in an online environment, so they ended up choosing one-sided teaching.

"How might we help students and teachers to overcome difficulties due to COVID-19, not only between cohorts but also between students and teachers?"

Implementation

System Architecture

We implemented our website by hard-coding, using some libraries and APIs.

For visualizing analyses from mood trackers, we used wordcloud and Chart.js library. We also added the Face API from Microsoft Cognitive Service, which classifies faces in images into eight emotions.

To classify Korean sentences, we used KoNLPy, which analyzes Korean words, and scikit-learn, for building machine learning models.

Implementation

Implementing into a Website

To create a website based on our design works, we used Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript on Django framework, using Visual Studio Code.

Implementation

Implementing a Machine Learning Model

I collected 36,338 sentences that were classified as negative (0) or positive (1). 70% of the sentences were used for training, and the rest were used for testing.

To classify new sentences into two groups, I used the logistic regression algorithm. As a result, the accuracy of the model was 89.17%.

Reflections

Checklist before Releasing

Privacy

The mood tracking feature encourages students to share their thoughts and express themselves, which can bring privacy issues to real users. A thorough review of how the personal data will be used and how it will be encoded is needed before releasing the product.

Clients' Needs

As Be_com is intended to be used by the whole class, the clients would not be individuals, but organizations: education offices, schools, and classes. Expected needs for organizations would be an easy-to-use admin tool to manage classes and students, charging no extra work to teachers and counselors, and integration with learning tools that are in use.

Reflections

What can be Improved?

Due to the time and resources our team had, there were some constraints regarding technical implementation, which had effects on features and designs. If I had more time or had experienced developers in the team, I would have improved on the following parts.

Detailed Analysis

I believe that the analysis of the mood tracking feature can go deeper! For now, words are only classified into two categories: positive and negative due to the lack of a detailed dataset. However, to provide a more elaborate analysis, more feelings can be added: depression, surprise, or sadness. I wish to expand analyses into supporting the mental health of students. For example, the system can detect depression or deliver appropriate words of sympathy and comfort from what the students have written.

Mobile Platform

For students, smartphones are more accessible and usable compared to desktops. While we could not develop mobile applications because our team members had experience in only web development, we made it responsive to support mobile views. However, considering the characteristics of Be_com, a mobile platform is still suitable to support real-time notifications features and engagement for students.
